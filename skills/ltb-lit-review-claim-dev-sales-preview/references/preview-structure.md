# Lit Review + Claim Dev Sales Preview — Deck Structure

A substantive-but-condensed preview (aim ~9–12 slides) — meatier than a teaser, lighter than the full 15-slide pitch. Output is a **clickable PDF**, 16:9, on the current LTB brand system (see brand-system.md), in website language (Science Intelligence™ · Science Story™ · Science Studio™; "Science leads, creativity amplifies"). Build as brand-styled HTML slides, then export to a clickable PDF with hyperlinks preserved (PMIDs → PubMed). The **claims section is the centerpiece** — that's what a prospect most wants to see.

Non-negotiable disclaimers:
- On every science/claims slide, a small footer: **"Preview · no brand input · findings not validated."**
- On claims, an asterisk note: **"Illustrative & directional — subject to MLR and legal review before any use."**

## Slide arc

1. **Cover** — logo top-left; eyebrow "SCIENCE MARKETING ENGINE · LIT REVIEW™ + CLAIM DEV™ PREVIEW"; "[Brand]" large; subline "A preview of the science we'd uncover — and the claims it supports"; 3pt rule; "Prepared by Let There Be · [Month Year]". Mitosis gradient blobs.
2. **Why this matters** (short) — the science behind [ingredient] moves faster than brands capture; here's an unbiased look at what the evidence already supports and could let you say.
3. **The territories we explored** — name the 2–3 strategic angles you chased (e.g., competitive vs. [rival], an underserved population, a high-value symptom). One line each on why. This frames the rest.
4–5. **Spotlight studies** (1–2 slides, organized by territory) — the evidence hero. Table: `Year | Study (author + linked PMID/DOI) | Key finding | Design | Direction`. Direction = Pivotal / Positive / Mixed / Note (colored dot). ~4–6 rows total across the strongest finds. One framing line per territory. Footer disclaimer.
6. **How the science becomes claims** (brief) — a light, confidence-building slide: every claim we surface is research-backed, scoped to your regulatory class, and written to survive MLR. Three short pillars (research-backed · regulatory-friendly · rooted in real needs). Keep it to one slide.
7–8. **The claims — what you could own** (1–2 slides, THE centerpiece) — 4–6 illustrative claims across the territories, developed per `claim-development.md`. Each claim card:
   - **Tier tag** (DTC-ready / DTC + HCP / HCP hero)
   - short **name** ("The pediatric gold standard")
   - **whitespace/differentiation + risk** read ("5★ whitespace · low risk")
   - the **claim in quotes** with an asterisk
   - **"Ownable:"** one-liner naming the study/authority behind it
   - where evidence is early, say so inline ("peripheral evidence currently preclinical")
   Mix science-forward and positioning-forward claims. Lead line: "A sample of what a full run surfaces — illustrative, not exhaustive." Asterisk disclaimer at the bottom.
9. **What the full engagement adds** — the preview used no brand input and skipped the systematic review, MLR alignment, and consumer testing. The full path: **Science Intelligence™** (complete lit review + MLR-ready claim set + evidence workbook) → **Science Story™** (claim clusters + narratives, consumer-tested) → **Science Studio™** (video, MOA, ad-to-education, influencer kits). This is the value gap.
10. **The ask / next step** — confident close: "Let's uncover the full picture for [Brand]." One line on engaging (a scoped Lit Review + Claim Dev, or a Claims Lab season) + next step.

One idea per slide, generous #F0F0F0 space, gradient reserved for one accent per slide.

## Study-table direction coding
Pivotal (dark green/teal dot) · Positive (teal) · Mixed (purple) · Note (grey).

## Claim depth (why this is not "too light")
Do real claim development on the preview: derive each claim from a specific study/authority, assign a tier, a whitespace/differentiation read, and a regulatory-risk sense — following `claim-development.md`. 4–6 claims across 2–3 territories is the target. The claims must feel like genuine, ownable opportunities the prospect hadn't articulated — that is what sells the engagement.
