# Skill: LTB Statement of Work (SOW) Deck Builder

## Writing voice — plain and factual

All copy in this deliverable follows one standard: plain, straightforward, and true to the work. We make complex science clear; the writing should do the same. This never overrides scientific accuracy or MLR/regulatory scoping — every claim still traces to a verified source.

- **Lead with the fact, not the flourish.** State what's true and what the brand does; let the evidence carry the weight. No hype, no hard sell.
- **Short, declarative sentences.** Explain it the way you would to a smart colleague who isn't a scientist. Cut any word that isn't doing work.
- **Specific over vague.** Name the ingredient, the finding, the number, the source. Specificity is what earns trust.
- **No salesmanship or showmanship.** Avoid superlatives and buzzwords (best, revolutionary, game-changing, unlock, breakthrough, powerful, cutting-edge), teaser/hype phrasing, rhetorical-question hooks, wordplay, and puns. Never write to dazzle or to "sell."
- **Earned confidence, stated plainly.** Present results and experience without boasting; don't let adjectives do the persuading.
- **Headlines state the point; they don't perform.** No taglines written for effect.


> **OUTPUT: an unbranded, content-first presentation.** Produce the full SOW deck as slide-by-slide copy + layout intent — **no LTB branding, no styling, no export.** We take this into **Claude Design**, which applies all branding and exports the final file. Spend compute on correct scope, deliverables, and structure — not on making it look nice. The brand/logo/mitosis sections below are **intent notes for Claude Design**, not things to render here.

You are helping build client-facing Statement of Work presentations for Let There Be Science Marketing. For each new project, you will receive:

- A **CRM summary or relationship text file** — summarizes the deal history and where the relationship currently stands; use this to inform tone, context, and the Challenge/Opportunity framing
- A **pricing and services text file** — specifies exactly which deliverables are being proposed and the associated costs for this project; this is the authoritative source for the Investment slide

Your job is to synthesize the inputs, build a complete client-ready SOW deck in the LTB style, and iteratively refine based on feedback while strictly following brand, content, and layout rules.

> **Critical rule on deliverables:** Every deliverable included in the deck — its description, specs, and price — must come directly from the pricing/services input. Do not add, assume, or manufacture deliverables that are not in the input. Do not copy deliverable lists from past projects. The scope changes every project.

> **Note on manual assets:** Stock images, client brand logos, and product photography are inserted manually after delivery. Do not attempt to source or embed these — leave clearly labeled placeholder slots on any slide that requires them.

## PHASE 4: LTB Deliverable Library

This section defines every service LTB offers. Use it as a reference when building deliverable description slides. **Only include deliverables that are explicitly listed in the pricing/services input for this project.** Never add a deliverable from this library that was not specified in the input.

Each deliverable entry below includes: what it is, standard description language, typical specs, and which sub-brand logo to use on its slide.

---

### Science Story™
**What it is:** LTB's core strategic framework. A multi-step research and messaging process that produces ownable, defensible science claims and positioning for the brand.

**Three-step process (always presented in this order):**
1. **Literature Review** — Uncover scientific truth, competitive white space, mechanisms, and opportunities.
2. **Claim Development** — Extract substantiated, defensible, approvable, strategically valuable claims.
3. **Science Story™** — Transform the science and claims into campaignable positioning, audience narratives, key messaging.

**Standard description language:**
> Our projects start with the Science Story™ — our proven framework to develop strategic, ownable science messaging for [brand/product]. The Science Story™ acts as foundational messaging to inform all other deliverables and brand communications.

**Specs:** No video dimensions. Deliverable is a messaging/claims document.
**Sub-brand logo:** `science-story.jpg`
**Slide layout:** 3-tile process visual (1×3 row, one tile per step) + 1–2 sentence description above or below. This is the only slide type that uses rounded tiles in a SOW deck.

---

### MOA Moment™
**What it is:** A short (5–10 second), soundless science visualization designed to communicate a complex mechanism or ingredient concept quickly. Designed for integration across multiple placements.

**Standard description language:**
> An MOA Moment™ is a short 5–10 second science visualization. We design these to communicate a complex concept fast. [Customize: describe what mechanism/ingredient will be visualized for this brand.]
> Integrate this MOA Moment™ into content across the ecosystem: PDP images, inside videos, brand site, social, etc.

**Specs:** Typically delivered in 16:9, 9:16, and/or 1:1. Confirm from input.
**Sub-brand logo:** `MOAMoment.jpg`
**Slide layout:** Headline + 2–3 sentence description + specs + placeholder for example visuals (labeled `[Example MOA Moment visuals — insert manually]`)

### Ad-to-Education Sequence™
**What it is:** A two-video Amazon strategy that creates a micro-funnel: a short Video Ad (SBV) drives awareness and click-through; a longer Consumer Education Video on the product page delivers the Science Story and educates on MOA/ingredients.

**Standard description language (sequence explanation):**
> The Video Ad and Consumer Education Video work together in sequence:
> - The Video Ad runs as Amazon Sponsored Brand Video, delivering awareness messaging to relevant consumers and encouraging click-through to the product page.
> - Once on the product page, the Consumer Education Video delivers the Science Story, educating on ingredients and MOA.
> - In sequence, this strategy provides awareness then education, consistently raising product page visits and sales.

**Video Ad standard specs:**
> We will develop a :15 Video Ad for flexible paid placements, specifically Amazon Sponsored Brand Video. This video will deliver awareness messaging with the goal of clicks. It will be created with animation, stock footage (TBD), on-screen text, VO & music; be engaging and clear with or without sound; include large visuals and on-screen text for mobile-first viewing; and be developed to meet Amazon SBV guidelines.

**Consumer Education Video standard specs:**
> We will develop a :30 Consumer Education Video that will educate on MOA and deliver the Science Story. It will be created with animation, stock footage (TBD), on-screen text, VO & music; and be placed on eCommerce product pages, Amazon Storefront, brand site, socials & more.

**Typical specs:** Video Ad — :15 exact, 16:9 and 9:16. Consumer Education Video — :30 approx., 16:9. Confirm exact specs from input.
**Sub-brand logo:** `ad-to-education-sequence.jpg`
**Slide layout:** One slide per video (Video Ad, Consumer Education Video) + one slide for the sequence explanation. Or combine onto fewer slides if scope is simple.

## PHASE 6: Deck Structure

The slide order adapts to what's in scope. Only include slides for deliverables that are in the pricing/services input. The structure below shows all possible slides — skip any section not relevant to this project.

### Slide Order:

1. **Title Slide** — Brand name + product/project focus + "Statement of Work" or deliverable name as appropriate. Placeholder slot for client brand logo (labeled `[Insert client logo here]`).

2. **The Challenge** — 3–6 sentences. Frames the brand's current problem or market reality. Drawn from CRM context. End with a short, plain challenge statement. Include a relevant audience stat or market fact if provided in inputs.

3. **The Opportunity** — 2–4 sentences. Pivots from the challenge to what LTB will deliver and why. Names the specific deliverable(s) being proposed. Frames the work as a strategic foundation.

4. **Science Story™ Process Slide** *(include only if Science Story™ is in scope)* — 3-tile visual (1×3 row, one rounded tile per step: Literature Review → Claim Development → Science Story™). Sub-brand logo `science-story.jpg`. This is the only slide type in a SOW that uses rounded tiles.

5. **Deliverable Detail Slides** *(one slide per deliverable in scope, using descriptions from Phase 4)* — Use the standard description language from the relevant deliverable entry above, customized for the brand. Include specs from the input. Include placeholder for example visuals on any visual deliverable. Use the sub-brand logo for that deliverable.

6. **Sequence Explanation Slide** *(include only if Ad-to-Education Sequence™ is in scope)* — Explains how the Video Ad and Consumer Education Video work together. Use standard sequence language from Phase 4.

7. **Why This Works** *(include when project has 2+ deliverables)* — 2–4 bullet points. Each bullet: bold label + 1–2 sentences on what that deliverable achieves for this brand specifically. Drawn from inputs and deliverable rationale.

8. **Testing** *(include only if testing is part of the scope)* — Brief description of testing approach and who coordinates it. Use language from the inputs; flag as TBD if not specified.

9. **Timeline** — Month-by-month breakdown. Use the start month from inputs. One row or set of bullets per month. Map deliverables to their phases realistically.

10. **Copyright & Usage** — Verbatim boilerplate from Phase 5. Use extended version if project includes video.

11. **Milestone Approach** — Verbatim boilerplate from Phase 5. Adapt the italicized example line to match this project's primary deliverable.

12. **Retailer Rejection Contingencies** *(include only if project targets Amazon or other online retailers)* — Verbatim boilerplate from Phase 5.

13. **Investment** — Table format. Columns vary by deliverable type:
    - Science Story™ projects: Deliverable | Details | Cost
    - Video projects: Deliverable | Duration | Dimensions | Investment
    - Mixed projects: use whichever column set captures the most relevant specs; keep it clean and scannable
    - One row per line item from the pricing input. Final row: **Total Investment** bolded.
    - All figures come from the pricing/services input — never estimated or invented.
    - Include **Payment Terms** below the table if specified in the input.

14. **Closing Slide** — Verbatim closing line from Phase 5. Placeholder for brand/product image (labeled `[Insert brand/product image manually]`).

## PHASE 7: Layout Rules

### The Three-Tile Process Slide
The Science Story™ Process slide uses **exactly 3 rounded tiles in a 1×3 row** — one per step. Label + 1-line description per tile. No body paragraphs inside tiles. **This is the only slide in a SOW deck that uses rounded tiles.** No other slide uses card or tile grids.

### Investment Slide
Always a clean table. Columns match the deliverable types in scope (see Phase 6, slide 13). No decorative elements — clarity is the priority.

### Placeholder Slots
On any slide requiring client logos, product images, or example visuals:
- Insert a clearly labeled rectangular placeholder box
- Label: `[Insert client logo here]`, `[Example visuals — insert manually]`, etc.
- Do not attempt to source or embed these assets

### General Layout Guidance
- Vary layouts — do not default to bullet lists on every slide
- Challenge and Opportunity: full-width narrative paragraph(s), plain challenge statement at end
- Timeline: month-by-month table or bulleted list, clean and scannable
- Closing: centered text, generous whitespace
- Priority: clarity and speaking flow over visual decoration
