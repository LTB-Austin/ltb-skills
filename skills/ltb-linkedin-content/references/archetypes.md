# LTB LinkedIn Post Archetypes

Five archetypes, each with its pattern, structure, and a real example pulled from LTB's posting history. Read the one you're writing before drafting.

---

## 1. Video Showcase

**The workhorse.** Most LTB posts are this: a past or finished project video, framed so the science is the hook. Serves "stay top of mind" + "educate."

**Structure:**
1. Hook — a consumer problem, a surprising science fact, or a "did you know?" question.
2. One or two sentences explaining what the video does / the science behind it.
3. Name the client and product; tag with @ if cleared.
4. Soft CTA (watch, link) optional.

**Two reliable hooks:**
- *Problem-first:* "Consumers buy on Amazon to shop fast — don't slow them down with long text descriptions."
- *Curiosity-first:* "Ever heard of Leaky Gut? Learn what it is and how to help improve it in this video we made with @WholisticMatters."

**Real example:**
> Consumers buy on Amazon and other eCommerce sites to shop fast and get products fast. Don't slow them down with long, segmented text descriptions. Let There Be created this eCommerce video for @Oystershell's RID brand so that, when lice strike, parents quickly can learn exactly what product they need and exactly how to use it.

**"Make it sciency" move:** Instead of "we did a great project with [client]," teach the mechanism. "Did you know how Advil works? Most people think X — it actually does Y." This turns a portfolio clip into an educational post.

## 2. Partnership & Momentum

New client, collaboration, or launch. Serves "celebrate momentum." Signals LTB keeps winning great work.

**Structure:**
1. Announcement, warm and direct. An emoji flag (🚨) is acceptable here.
2. What the work is and why it matters for that brand's mission.
3. Forward-looking, optimistic close.

**Real example:**
> We're excited to announce a new partnership with PetIQ! Together, we'll create science-driven brand stories and Ad to Education Sequences for many of PetIQ's pet health products — from treats, to supplements, to OTC treatments — helping pet parents make smarter choices and supporting PetIQ's mission to advance pet health with science-backed products.

**Always flag in NOTES:** confirm the client has approved being named publicly.

---

## 3. Case Study & Results

A project with a measurable outcome. The highest-value archetype for "educate + convert." Best as a swipeable carousel.

**Structure (carousel = one idea per card):**
1. **Hook card:** the result, up front. "This Amazon video increased sales 10%."
2. **Problem card:** what the client was up against.
3. **Approach card:** what LTB did.
4. **Result card:** the number, with a plain-language note on how it was measured.
5. **CTA card:** soft invitation.

**Real example (caption form):**
> This Amazon BasicCare video increased sales of Hair Regrowth Treatment by 10%. Adding short explainer-style videos to your Amazon product page not only boosts page SEO, but also delivers an engaging message to consumers ABOVE the fold — without making them read long product descriptions.
>
> [Sales increased measured over 6-month period after posting video. Study conducted with Let There Be, Amazon, and Perrigo.]

**Rule:** Never invent the number. If the user didn't supply it, write the structure and flag the missing stat in NOTES.

## 4. Conference & BTS

CHPA, SLS, NACDS presence; booths; behind-the-scenes from shoots or the studio. Serves "stay top of mind" — these are highly repostable because people tag friends and say "I'll be there too."

**Structure:**
1. Where LTB is / was, and why it matters.
2. A human, in-the-room detail (a talk, a booth, a conversation).
3. Invitation to connect ("come say hi," "let's meet at…").

**Tone:** more personal than the science posts. Often ghostwritten for Alex's personal page. Reference the credibility the presence lends a small agency.

---

## 5. Team & Culture

Promotions, new hires, new roles, and stories about LTB's international team. Serves "celebrate" + builds brand connection. Rare and worth doing well — culture is a differentiator.

**Structure:**
1. Celebratory announcement (🚨 acceptable).
2. The person, their role, and the specific value they bring.
3. Warm congratulations / human close.

**Real example:**
> Please join us in celebrating 🚨 A New Role at LTB 🚨 We're thrilled to announce Austin Miller's transition from contractor to full-time team member as our Content & AI Strategist! In this newly created role, Austin will own our Science Story™ methodology, transforming complex science into strategic clarity for our clients. His work blends scientific research, storytelling, and AI integration. Congratulations, Austin!

**Culture angle:** LTB has a genuinely international, collaborative team — stories about how the team works across the world build brand warmth and are rare in this industry.
