# LTB Consumer Test Package

You are preparing the bridge between LTB's creative work and the consumer-testing partner. The partner runs the field study and the statistics; your job is to hand them **clean, coded, compliance-clean stimuli** and a crisp brief, so the test measures the idea — not our formatting.

## Inputs
- The Science Story stimuli: the candidate headlines, narratives, and consumer-facing claim lines (from the ltb-science-story deliverable).
- Each stimulus's source direction/cluster and register (science-forward vs positioning-forward).
- Product regulatory class + the required disclaimer.
- What the client and team want to learn (the decision the test should settle).

## Build

1. **Neutralize & code every stimulus.** Internal direction names are for us, not consumers. Assign neutral codes: headlines H1, H2…; narratives N1, N2…; claims C1, C2…. Keep an internal key mapping codes → direction so we can read results back.
2. **Make stimuli comparable.** Narratives being compared should be similar length/format; claims phrased in parallel structure. The test should isolate the *idea*, not word count or polish.
3. **Keep it compliance-clean.** Every claim stimulus stays within the regulatory class and carries its disclaimer. Never send language we couldn't actually use — an unusable winner wastes the test.
4. **Build the stimulus workbook (.xlsx)** — one row per stimulus:
   `Code | Type (headline/narrative/claim) | Text | Source direction (internal) | Register | Disclaimer | KPI battery to apply | Notes`
   Plus a separate "Key" tab mapping codes to internal direction names (for our eyes; not shared raw with respondents).
5. **Write the one-page brief** (.docx or top tab): the decision the test should settle · audience definition (category buyers, decision role, quotas) · the stimulus inventory (counts by type) · primary KPI = persuasion / purchase intent (Top-2-Box) · diagnostic KPIs = believability, uniqueness, relevance, clarity · any specific comparisons we care about.

## Rules
- Reference the full testing methodology (designs, sample sizes, scoring, thresholds, AI synthetic pretest) in the **Science Story Master Guide, Part 4** — don't re-derive statistics here; the partner owns the field method.
- Keep our creative judgment and the consumer's verdict separate: we supply neutral stimuli, the test decides.
- Never include a stimulus that isn't defensible/compliant, even to "see how it tests."
- Use the `xlsx` skill for the sheet and `docx` for the brief.
