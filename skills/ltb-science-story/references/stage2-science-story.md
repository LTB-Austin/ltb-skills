# Stage 2 — Polished Science Story™ (post client direction + built test-ready)

Purpose: take the client's chosen direction and turn it into the polished, substantiated Science Story — and structure it so the narratives, headlines, and claims can go straight to the consumer-testing partner. The narrative leads; the claims and sources are the substantiation behind it.

## Reading the client feedback first
Extract before building: preferred cluster/direction · cross-cluster claim requests ("bring in the hydration claim from cluster 1") · topics to explore · tone/framing preferences · guardrails (anything flagged for MLR or to avoid). Document your interpretation in working notes. Honor the preferred direction as the backbone; pull in claims from other clusters only if explicitly requested.

## Slide order
1. **Title** — Brand + "Science Story™, Claims & Narratives".
2. **Process overview** — engine in current site language.
3. **Challenge** — carried from Stage 1, lightly refined.
4. **A Note** — the "compass, not a script" framing (see standard language below).
5. **Science Story™ — Cover** — a brand-affirmative headline that resolves the challenge and names the brand; one-line subhead restating the promise in plain language. Lead with what the brand *is/does*.
6. **Science Story™ — Body** — narrative (left, 3–5 paragraphs, ~200–300 words, product named naturally, ends on a bolded callback to the headline) + **Claims Used** table (right): Claim Name | Core Idea. When testing multiple narratives, give each its own Body slide.
7. **Activation Roadmap** *(optional)* — Activation | Science Story™ messaging to build from. Include only if activations are specified.
8. **MLR Readiness Checklist** *(optional)* — items to confirm before approval.
9. **Polished Claims — definition** — "Claims are distilled, defensible statements…" with a POLISHED label.
10. **Claim slides (per claim):** Claim Body slide + Source Card slide(s).
11. **Test Plan** — the test-readiness section (see testing-readiness.md).
12. **Next steps** — 3 steps with dates.

## A Note slide (standard language)
1. **The text inside each claim** is not final consumer copy — think of it as a compass, not a script. Each claim is a scientific pillar of the Science Story.
2. **Claim Opportunities** are how those pillars show up in consumer-facing copy.
3. As we move into the final **Science Story** and **Activations** ([list deliverables in scope]), we shape every message around your MLR team's input and work with you to get items approved.

## Claim Body slide
- Multicolor claim mark left of the headline; headline large/bold with a divider beneath.
- Full claim body (verbatim from the claim set).
- **Claim Opportunities:** numbered list of 3 consumer-facing claim lines (rules below).
- LTBFavorite marker (bottom-left) if applicable — 2–3 per deck, selective.

## Source Card slide(s)
- Claim headline repeated as context header + divider.
- Two source cards per slide, each bordered: Title · Author/Institution · Published (year) · Direct Quote (verbatim, in quotes) · Applicability Note (2–3 sentences connecting evidence → the claim it enables). >2 sources → more slides, 2 per slide.

## Claim Opportunity writing rules
Consumer-facing (not clinical); name the product in ≥1 of the 3; grounded only in what the sources support; direct, specific, and plain (not hedged, not hyped); vary sentence structure across the 3; the most data-specific line comes last when a hard number exists. Every line carries the product's required disclaimer.

## Process overview (current site language)
01 Challenge Framing · 02 Curiosity & Exploration (AI-assisted; optional human brainstorm) · 03 Claim Development (from verified science) · 04 Claim Clusters & Narratives · 05 Consumer Testing · 06 Science Studio™ Activation.

## Next steps (standard)
Step 1 — Strategic Alignment: confirm Science Story direction. Step 2 — Consumer Testing: test narratives/headlines/claims, refine on results. Step 3 — Scripts & Art Styles: develop creative & Ad-to-Education. Dates as provided.
