# Stage 1 — Claim Cluster Selection Deck

Purpose: give the client 2–4 meaningfully different **strategic directions** to choose between. Each direction is a **Claim Cluster** — a bundle of claims + a narrative — not a loose list of facts. The client picks ONE direction (and may flag edits); that decision starts Stage 2.

## What a Claim Cluster is
- **Strategic direction** — researcher-named, free-text (e.g., "The Inside-Out Story," "Start-on-Day-1 Urgency"). Not from a fixed menu.
- **Narrative Anchor** — one line stating what this direction does for the brand.
- **Creative narrative** — 2–4 sentences of the story this cluster substantiates (the thing consumers would feel). This is what gets tested later.
- **2–4 member claims** — mutually reinforcing, drawn from the verified claim set. Mix registers deliberately: some **science-forward** ("this ingredient does X"), some **positioning-forward** (uses the science to advance a market angle). Note each claim's register.
- **Coherence** — the claims fit together and don't contradict.

## Slide order
1. **Title** — Brand + "Science Story™ — Claim Clusters" + project context.
2. **Process overview** *(optional)* — the engine in current site language (Prove → Persuade → Produce; Science Intelligence → Science Story → Science Studio).
3. **The challenge** — 1–2 sentences, grounded in the science and the client's brief/feedback.
4. **What is a Claim Cluster?** — the definition above, in plain client-facing language.
5. **[Cluster name] — cover** — announcement slide: direction name + Narrative Anchor + 1–2 sentence framing.
6. **[Cluster name] — claims** — all claims in the cluster on ONE slide, rounded-card grid (2×2 for 4, 1×3 for 3, 1×2 for 2). Card = claim headline (6–10 words) + body + citation `— Author, Year, Journal` + a small register tag (Science / Positioning).
7. *(repeat 5–6 per cluster)*
8. **Side-by-side compare** *(recommended)* — the Top 3 directions in three columns (direction · anchor · what it's best for) so the client can choose.
9. **Next steps** — client selects a direction and flags edits → Stage 2 polishes it, writes the full narrative(s), and prepares consumer testing.

## Card & layout rules
- Cover + claims slides always appear as a pair; never separate them or mix claims from different clusters on one slide.
- Never give a single claim its own full slide at Stage 1.
- Claim headline/body/citation come verbatim from the verified claim set — never invent a claim or a source. If it's not in the workbook/claim set, it doesn't exist.
- Rounded cards appear only on the process-overview and cluster-claims slides. Everything else uses standard LTB layouts (full-width narrative, split, single highlighted card, numbered next steps).
