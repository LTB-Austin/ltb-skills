# Lit Review & Claim Dev Deck — Structure & Slide Archetypes

Output is always **PPTX**, 16:9 (13.333in × 7.5in). Build with the `pptx` skill. Follow `brand-system.md` for all color/type. One idea per slide, lots of `#f0f0f0` breathing room. Study tables and claim tables are the backbone — they must be clean, aligned, and readable.

Use **current site language** for section names and taglines (Science Intelligence™, Lit Review™, Claim Dev™, "Science leads, creativity amplifies," Prove/Persuade/Produce). Do not import deck-specific coinages from older examples.

## Full flow (adapt count to the project)

**Front matter**
1. **Cover** — logo top-left; eyebrow "SCIENCE MARKETING ENGINE"; product name very large (with ® if applicable); "Lit Review™ & Claim Dev™"; 3pt black rule; "Prepared for [Client] · Prepared by Let There Be · [Month Year]". Mitosis gradient blobs bleeding off two corners.
2. **The opportunity** — the "why": traditional innovation is slow/expensive; science marketing is faster. Short.
3. **Contents** — numbered sections with page refs.
4. **The model** — "Science leads. Creativity amplifies." The three-stage engine (Prove → Persuade → Produce) with arrows.
5. **The engagement / scope** — what this review covered for this brand.
6. **The formula map** — SKUs across the top, actives down the side; shows which active is in which product.
7. **Legend — how to read this report** — evidence-direction key, the throughline, formula coding.

**Part I — the evidence (Lit Review)**
8. **Part divider** — "[N] studies across [M] actives — read, mapped, and weighed by evidence direction and design quality."
9. **Evidence base at a glance** — one big hero number (studies reviewed) + direction breakdown (Positive / Mixed / Null counts) + a "studies by active" list with mitosis-gradient bars. Footnote the design mix (RCTs, meta-analyses).
10. **Evidence matrix** — grid of actives (rows) × outcomes (columns) with a mark where evidence lands. The "where the evidence lands" overview.
11. **Per active — repeat for each ingredient:**
    - **Active header** — "ACTIVE 0N · [SKU]" eyebrow; ingredient name in ink + a grey descriptor ("/ the clinical core"); a hero stat top-right (e.g., "42% shorter colds · Mossad 1996"); one-line thesis.
    - **Evidence table(s)** — columns: `Yr | Authors | Study & venue | Design | Finding | Key result | Relevance to [Brand]`. Finding uses a colored dot + label (Positive / Positive PIVOTAL / Mixed / Null / Note). **Highlight the pivotal row** with a soft mint fill. Footer: "[Active] · 1 of K · studies 1–4 of [total]". Split across 2 slides if >4–5 studies.
    - **Spotlight** — one great study, deep. Either a symptom-by-symptom / outcome-by-outcome results table (brand vs placebo vs significance) or a single killer figure. Always include the honest guardrail line ("No significant difference: … — a useful guardrail on what not to claim"). Footer cites the study.
12. **What the literature tells us** — synthesis: each active earns a specific, defensible lane (one line per active).

**Part II — the claims (Claim Dev)**
13. **Part divider** — "From evidence to ownable claims — what's approved today, where the new opportunities are, and the guardrails."
14. **How we extract claims** — the three tests every claim direction clears + the internal MLR review reality.
15. **The baseline** — the client's approved claim set today (claim family | approved language | required context). Reference the approval doc code if known.
16. **Already in market** — current live claims by ingredient (from the client site), so every new opportunity is clearly built on top of it.
17. **Guardrails — claims to keep off the table** — the non-negotiable lines with ✕ and the reason each is off-limits (e.g., null evidence, regulatory class). This is a trust-builder; do not skip it.
18. **The opportunity field** — hero count of opportunities identified.
19. **Opportunities — repeat per SKU / active grouping:** table with columns `Opportunity | Working language — not yet approved | Science basis | What's needed`. Put the working language in a soft grey rounded chip. "What's needed" states the substantiation/regulatory step (e.g., "confirm per-symptom results meet substantiation — no new studies needed"). Standard disclaimer as a footnote with the asterisk that appears in the working language.

**Close**
20. **The roadmap** — the four-stage engine; where the brand stands today and how validated claims flow into Science Story™ (clusters → narratives → consumer testing) and Science Studio™.
21. **Closing** — "SCIENCE LEADS. CREATIVITY AMPLIFIES." + "Prepared by Let There Be for [Client] · [Month Year]".

## Finding-direction coding (matches the evidence workbook)
POSITIVE PIVOTAL (dark green/teal dot, bold) · POSITIVE (teal dot) · MIXED (purple dot) · NULL (red dot — kept as a guardrail, never hidden) · NOTE (grey). Gradient bars for "studies by active" use the mitosis gradient.

## Rules
- Every study row and claim traces to a real citation from the evidence workbook. Never invent a study or statistic.
- Show NULL/negative evidence and guardrails openly — it is what makes the claim set defensible and is a selling point of the LTB method.
- Claims are scoped to the product's regulatory class and carry the required disclaimer. Working (unapproved) language is always visibly labeled "not yet approved."
- Spotlight studies give the strongest evidence room to stand on its own — pick the clearest, most ownable finding per active.
