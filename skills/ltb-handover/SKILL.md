# LTB Project Handover

## Writing voice — plain and factual

All copy in this deliverable follows one standard: plain, straightforward, and true to the work. We make complex science clear; the writing should do the same. This never overrides scientific accuracy or MLR/regulatory scoping — every claim still traces to a verified source.

- **Lead with the fact, not the flourish.** State what's true and what the brand does; let the evidence carry the weight. No hype, no hard sell.
- **Short, declarative sentences.** Explain it the way you would to a smart colleague who isn't a scientist. Cut any word that isn't doing work.
- **Specific over vague.** Name the ingredient, the finding, the number, the source. Specificity is what earns trust.
- **No salesmanship or showmanship.** Avoid superlatives and buzzwords (best, revolutionary, game-changing, unlock, breakthrough, powerful, cutting-edge), teaser/hype phrasing, rhetorical-question hooks, wordplay, and puns. Never write to dazzle or to "sell."
- **Earned confidence, stated plainly.** Present results and experience without boasting; don't let adjectives do the persuading.
- **Headlines state the point; they don't perform.** No taglines written for effect.


You are assembling the finale of an engine project: the single, navigable document a client's brand team can load into their systems and that lets LTB's **Science Studio** move straight into scripting and art. It pulls together everything the engine produced and everything that was approved — the science, the claims, the Science Story, the consumer-test results, and the production briefs.

**Output: one navigable .docx** with a clickable table of contents. Offer a PDF export. Keep it thoughtfully designed and genuinely easy to navigate — this is what represents the whole engagement.

## Inputs
Gather the approved outputs from the earlier stages (ask for what's missing):
- Evidence workbook (.xlsx) + spotlight studies (Science Intelligence).
- Approved claims by SKU, with expression levels, disclaimers, MLR status (Claim Dev).
- The approved Science Story: winning direction, narrative(s), claim cluster (Science Story stage).
- Consumer-test results, if available (winning stimuli + KPIs). If not yet run, mark that section pending.
- The activation deliverables in scope for Science Studio, plus brand/regulatory guardrails.
- Optional: LTB brand archive for the cover and section imagery.

## Build
1. **Load** `references/brand-system.md` (current palette/type; language from the current site) and `references/handover-structure.md` (section order + the Studio brief block).
2. **Assemble, don't regenerate.** Pull approved content from the real project outputs. Summarize the science inline; reference the workbook as the data appendix. Never invent a study, claim, or result.
3. **Build with the `docx` skill** — a clickable TOC (heading styles + TOC field), clean heading hierarchy, bookmarks for cross-references, brand-consistent styling. Then offer to render a PDF.
4. **Mark status everywhere** — Approved / In MLR / Not for use, with disclaimers shown. Only approved, in-scope content is presented as usable.
5. **Nail the Studio briefs.** For each approved deliverable, give scripting/art everything they need to start: approved narrative arc, must-appear claims (verbatim, with disclaimers), guardrails, key studies/figures to visualize, tone, mandatory legal language.

## Rules
- Single source of truth: every claim and figure traces to the project outputs; nothing fabricated.
- Only approved, in-scope material is usable; unapproved content is labeled and quarantined, not omitted silently.
- Current site engine language throughout. No "gem/gem set" language — claims and claim clusters only.
- The document should stand on its own: a new team member should be able to open it and understand the whole project.
