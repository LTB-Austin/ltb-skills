# Handover Document — Structure

One navigable .docx (offer a PDF export too). It is the single place a client's brand team — and LTB's Science Studio — can find everything the engine produced and everything that was approved. Thoughtfully designed, sectioned, and easy to load into the client's systems.

## Requirements
- **Navigable:** a clickable Table of Contents (heading styles + TOC field), consistent heading hierarchy, and a short section index so any team member lands where they need to.
- **Self-contained but linked:** summarize the science and claims inline; reference the full evidence workbook (.xlsx) as the data appendix rather than pasting every study.
- **On-brand:** brand-system.md — Lexend, ink on light, mitosis-gradient accents used sparingly, clean editorial layout.
- **Status-clear:** every claim/narrative marked Approved / In MLR / Not for use, with the required disclaimer shown.

## Section order
1. **Cover** — client + product line; "Science Marketing Engine — Project Handover"; prepared by Let There Be; date.
2. **How to use this document** — 3–4 lines: what's inside, who each section is for (brand team, MLR, creative), and where the source files live.
3. **Executive summary** — the approved strategic direction, the winning headline, the top 3 approved claims, and the consumer-test verdict in one page.
4. **The Science** — evidence-at-a-glance (study counts, direction mix); per-ingredient spotlight studies (the strongest, most ownable findings); a pointer to the full evidence workbook appendix. Keep it readable, not exhaustive.
5. **The Claims** — approved claims by SKU, at Consumer / HCP / MLR expression levels, each with supporting study, regulatory risk, disclaimer, and MLR status. Include the guardrails ("claims to keep off the table") so the client's team doesn't wander off-scope.
6. **The Science Story** — the approved narrative(s) and the claim cluster behind each; the Narrative Anchor; the claims used.
7. **Consumer Test Results** — what was tested (coded stimuli), what won and by how much (primary KPI + diagnostics), and the resulting refinements. If testing hasn't happened yet, mark the section "pending" rather than omitting it.
8. **Science Studio™ Briefs** — for each approved deliverable in scope (e.g., MOA Moment™, Ad-to-Education Sequence™, Influencer Science Kit™, a video): everything scripting/art needs to start — the approved narrative arc, the claims that must appear (with disclaimers), the guardrails, the key studies/figures to visualize, tone, and any mandatory legal language. This is the section that lets Science Studio move straight into scripting and art styles.
9. **Sources & Appendix** — numbered bibliography with live PubMed/DOI links; a link/reference to the evidence workbook and the source decks.

## Studio brief block (repeat per deliverable)
- Deliverable + format/length.
- Approved narrative arc (the story to tell).
- Claims that must appear (verbatim) + disclaimers.
- Guardrails (what not to say/show).
- Key studies/figures to visualize.
- Tone + any mandatory legal language.

## Rules
- Only approved, in-scope content goes in the handover as usable; anything unapproved is clearly labeled and quarantined.
- Every claim traces to a source; nothing invented. Pull from the actual project outputs (workbook, claim set, science story, test results).
- The handover reflects the current site's engine language (Science Intelligence™ · Science Story™ · Science Studio™; Prove/Persuade/Produce; "Science leads, creativity amplifies").
